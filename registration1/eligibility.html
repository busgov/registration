Employees

You’re an employee if you’re working as an apprentice, a trainee, a labourer, or trade assistant.

Generally, employees:

  • are supervised by an employer
  • are required to carry out their work in a particular way
  • earn a wage or salary
  • are entitled to pay leave
  • work set hours on a permanent, fixed term or casual basis
  • can’t subcontract work tasks given to them.

Employees are different to contractors, who set up businesses to hire out their services.

For more:

  • read about the differences between contractors and employees on the Tax Office website
  • check out our Independent contractors decision tool
  • Contact the Tax Office. You can book a free assistance visit, or call them on 13 28 66.

Trusts and self-managed super funds

Trusts

Using a trust structure for your business means that your business is held and controlled by for the benefit of others (the beneficiaries).

A trustee is responsible for everything in the trust, including income and losses, and must report income in a trust income tax return.

A trustee can be a company or a person.

Self-managed super funds

Self-managed super funds are a way of saving for your retirement. Most people contribute to a super fund that’s already been set up.

If you set up a self-managed super fund, you’re in charge. This means you decide where the fund invests its money and you are responsible for meeting super and tax laws.

Apprentices, trainees and other employees

You don’t need to register a business to work as an employee!

If your employer is asking you to register as a contractor when you’re really an employee, you can report the business to the ATO.

Overseas businesses

If your business operates overseas, you don’t need to register it in Australia, but you’ll still need to pay tax on income if you’re an Australian resident.

Find out more about how foreign business income is taxed by the ato.

Trusts and self-managed super funds

Trusts and self-managed super funds are complex to set up and you’ll need time and the right skills to do it properly. It's best to see a qualiﬁed, licensed professional to help you decide.

  • Visit the Tax Office website for more info if you’re thinking about self-managed super or starting a trust
  • Once you’re ready to go, come back to our website to register your self-managed super fund.

Employees

You’re an employee if you’re working as an apprentice, a trainee, a labourer, or trade assistant.

Generally, employees:

  • are supervised by an employer
  • are required to carry out their work in a particular way
  • earn a wage or salary
  • are entitled to paid leave
  • work set hours on a permanent, fixed term or casual basis
  • can’t subcontract work tasks given to them.

Employees are different to contractors, who set up businesses to hire out their services.

For more:

  • read about the differences between contractors and employees on the Tax Office website
  • check out our Independent contractors decision tool
  • Contact the Tax Office. You can book a free assistance visit, or call them on 13 28 66.

Location of your business

Your business is operating overseas if your main business operations are overseas and you are not trading goods and services in Australia.

Having overseas customers, or importing or exporting goods internationally, doesn’t mean your business is operating overseas.

In this context Australia, means:

  • States and internal Territories of Australia
  • The following external territories:
    • Norfolk Island
    • the Coral Sea Islands Territory
    • the Territory of Ashmore and Cartier Islands
    • the Territory of Christmas Island
    • the Territory of Cocos (Keeling) Islands
    • the Territory of Heard Island and the McDonald Islands
  • Australia's territorial waters (including the territorial waters surrounding the external Territories)
  • much of the waters contained in Australia's exclusive economic zone
  • the airspace above and the sea-bed and subsoil beneath Australia's waters

Non-profit organisations and self-managed super funds

Non-profit organisations

A non-profit (also known as not-for-profit) organisation does not operate for the profit or gain of its individual members, whether these gains would have been direct or indirect. This applies both while the organisation is operating and when it winds up.

A non-profit organisation does not mean that the organisation that hasn't made a profit. A non-profit organisation can still make a profit, but this profit must be used to carry out its purposes and must not be distributed to owners, members or other private people.

Due to the broad range of considerations that will affect how you register your not-for-profit organisation, the registration discovery tool does not cater for non-profit organisations. Visit the ATO website for more information on registering a non-profit organisation.

Once you understand which registrations you need for your non-profit organisation you can complete those registrations using the Ready to register service.

Self-managed super funds

Self-managed super funds are a way of saving for your retirement. Most people contribute to a super fund that’s already been set up.

If you set up a self-managed super fund, you’re in charge. This means you decide where the fund invests its money and you are responsible for meeting super and tax laws.

Due to the broad range of considerations that will affect how you register your self-managed super fund, the registration discovery tool does not cater for Self-managed super funds. Visit the ATO website for more information on Self-managed super funds.

Once you understand which registrations you need for your non-profit organisation you can complete those registrations using the Ready to register service.

Apprentices, trainees and other employees

You don’t need to get an Australian Business Number to work as an employee!

Employees are different to contractors, who set up businesses to hire out their services.

If you’re not sure whether you’re an employee or contractor, try our Independent contractors decision tool.

If your employer is asking you to register as a contractor when you’re really an employee, you can report the business to the Tax Office.

Overseas businesses

We don’t cover overseas businesses in this guide!

If your overseas business will sell goods or services in Australia, contact the Tax Office for guidance on what taxes you need to register for.

You can come back to our website to register for taxes, if required.

If you’re an Australian resident, you’ll still need to pay tax on business income you earn overseas. Find out more about how foreign business income is taxed by the ATO.

Non-profit organisations and self-managed super funds

Non-profit organisations and self-managed super funds are complex to set up and you’ll need time and the right skills to do it properly. It's best to see a qualiﬁed, licensed professional to help you decide.

  • Visit the Tax Office website for more info if you’re thinking about self-managed super or starting a non-profit organisation
  • Once you’re ready to go, come back to our website to register your self-managed super fund.

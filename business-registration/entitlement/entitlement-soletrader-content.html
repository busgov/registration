Have you started your business or taken steps to start it?

If you're just thinking about starting a business, you're not yet entitled to an ABN.

You're not entitled to an ABN

You need to have started your business, or taken steps to set up your business, to be entitled to an ABN (such as writing a business plan, applying for finance, buying equipment for your business, and so on).

This shows us that you're committed to starting your business.

Have a look at the Starting a business checklist for other common things people do when they set up a business, and come back when you've completed several steps.

What will your business do?

To get an ABN, we expect you to plan to make a profit or undertake ongoing business activities.

You're not entitled to an ABN

If your business activity isn't ongoing or repeated, or you're not planning to make a profit or gain, what you're doing could be more of a hobby than a business. Hobbies don't need ABNs. For more about this, read the Tax Office's Business or hobby page.

You can also read more about ABN eligibility on the Australian Business Register website.

Where will your business operate?

Will you do any of the following?

  • Deliver goods or make goods available in Australia to buyers
  • Remove goods from Australia
  • Supply services that are carried out in Australia
  • Import, install or assemble goods in Australia

You're entitled to an ABN

You're not entitled to an ABN

You're not entitled to an ABN because you're not operating in or supplying goods to Australia.

You can still continue with the form to register for GST.
